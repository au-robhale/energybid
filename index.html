The Regional Challenge

Energy Vulnerability

Regional communities face disproportionate energy costs, with households spending up to 12% of income on energy compared to 6% in metropolitan areas.

Economic Drain

Our region loses approximately $7M annually to external energy retailers, funds that could be reinvested in local communities.

Infrastructure Gap

With smart meter penetration at just 15-18%, our community lacks the tools to participate in the modern energy economy.

Capacity Constraints

Limited local expertise in energy technology creates barriers to adopting cost-saving innovations.

Blueprint for Regional Energy Revolution

NOVA Smart Region Incubator (SRI), UNE's innovation hub, presents a pioneering $445,000 regional energy transformation blueprint that aligns with New England's designation as a Renewable Energy Zone (REZ). This strategic initiative leverages proven economic multiplier effects, validated through Regional Australia Institute research showing every dollar retained generates 2.1x benefit in regional economies.

Current Challenge

Smart meter penetration at just 15-18% with electricity prices rising by 21.7% in 2022-23. Our community sends approximately $7 million annually to external energy retailers.

Strategic Solution

Through partnership with Automised Energy's proven technology platform and Armidale Regional Council's established green initiatives, including their Origin Zero agreement, we'll create a replicable model for regional NSW.

Project Investment

By retaining $400,000-$600,000 in local economies, we'll generate $1.05M in total annual economic benefit through validated 2.1x regional multiplier effects, while positioning our community to maximize benefits from the REZ investment.
